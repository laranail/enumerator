Forward-looking planning doc, drafted 2026-05-16 (pre-tag for v0.3.0).
Pulls the deferred items from
[`v0.3.0-scope.md`](v0.3.0-scope.md), the carry-overs in
[`.design/_private/NEXT-SESSION.md`](../_private/NEXT-SESSION.md), and

## Lessons carried in from v0.3.0 (apply in v0.4.0)

These three matter enough to call out at the top.

1. **Smoke-test before tag, always.** The v0.3.0 cross-project smoke
   test caught a fatal trait-method collision (`IsCacheKey::has()` vs
   `HasEnumeratorBehavior::has(target)`) that 800+ unit tests, full
   PHPStan, and Pint had not surfaced. The smoke test ran a real
   Laravel skeleton against `composer require laranail/enumerator`,
   not a Testbench harness. Every release cycle from here on out
   gets the same gate before tag.
2. **Pre-tag audit is cheap.** F1–F8 surfaced two real XSS issues
   (Icon raw render, wireModel attribute concatenation) plus a
   subtle locale-fallback bug (F8) inside the v0.3.0 freeze window.
   None of them blocked release. The audit pass is now part of the
   `audit-remediate.md` recipe; run it as a discrete phase between
   "validation green" and "tag".
3. **Coverage gate moves with the surface, not the calendar.**
   v0.3.0 ships at **83.99 %** lines (was 83.40 % at freeze, +0.59
   from PR-β's BladeViewHelpers / LaravelTranslatorAdapter push).
   The CI gate stays at `--min=83`. Don't bump the gate until the
   measured number sustains 85 %+ across two consecutive releases —
   v0.2.0 → v0.2.1 taught us that lesson hard.

## Estimating sequence

The "must-land before next tag" set is **PR-θ, PR-ι, PR-π** — they're
all cleanup/hardening. PR-κ (Infection floor) lands as soon as CI
data is available. The rest is feature-shaped and can slot in by
priority.

A reasonable v0.4.0 ship target: **PR-θ + PR-ι + PR-λ + PR-π +
PR-μ**, with PR-κ landing whenever the Infection data is ready
(possibly as v0.4.1). That's about 2-3 sessions of focused work, in
line with the v0.3.0 cycle's actual cost.

A v0.5.0 follow-up would naturally pick up PR-ν, PR-ξ, PR-ο, PR-ρ,
PR-σ.
